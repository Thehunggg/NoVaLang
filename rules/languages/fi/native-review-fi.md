# fi — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Finnish (fi-FI) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Finnish
language facts the project owner (not a Finnish speaker) cannot verify. None
block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-fi-01] Case endings across the 15 cases

Confirm a compact case paradigm (nominative/genitive/partitive + the 6 locatives)
for the first lesson's nouns, including vowel harmony and stem changes.

- [ ] Case forms correct for lesson-1 nouns.

## [N-fi-02] Partitive usage

Partitive (-a/-ä/-ta/-tä) has no Indo-European equivalent (partial/mass objects,
after numbers, negation). Confirm example sentences choose partitive vs
accusative correctly.

- [ ] Partitive vs accusative correct in examples.

## [N-fi-03] Consonant gradation

Confirm the strong/weak grade alternation (kk→k, pp→p, tt→t, t→d, k→∅, p→v) is
correct for lesson vocabulary and that the gradation type per word is right.

- [ ] Consonant gradation correct.

## [N-fi-04] Vowel harmony in suffixes

Confirm every suffix harmonizes with the stem (talossa vs metsässä) and that
neutral e/i words take the right harmony.

- [ ] Vowel harmony correct in all suffixed forms.

## [N-fi-05] Verb types + negation

Confirm verb-type classification and that negation uses the negative verb ei
conjugated for person (en/et/ei...) with the correct verb stem.

- [ ] Verb types + ei-negation correct.

## [N-fi-06] yleiskieli vs puhekieli boundary

Confirm example sentences are consistent yleiskieli (written standard) and that
any spoken-form annotations (puhekieli) are labeled, not mixed in.

- [ ] Consistent yleiskieli; puhekieli labeled if present.

## [N-fi-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences sound natural to a native (not literal),
case choice idiomatic, sinä/te appropriate.

- [ ] Sentences natural; case + address idiomatic.
