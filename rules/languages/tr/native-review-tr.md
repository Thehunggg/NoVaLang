# tr — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Turkish (tr-TR) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Turkish
language facts the project owner (not a Turkish speaker) cannot verify. None
block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-tr-01] Vowel harmony on lesson vocabulary suffixes

Confirm suffixes harmonize correctly (plural -ler/-lar, possessive, cases) for
the first lesson's words, including any loanword exceptions (kitap→kitabı).

- [ ] Vowel harmony correct on lesson-1 suffixed forms.

## [N-tr-02] Consonant mutation

Confirm final-consonant voicing (p→b, ç→c, t→d, k→ğ) applies/doesn't apply
correctly for lesson nouns (kitap→kitabı but at→atı).

- [ ] Consonant mutation correct (incl. non-mutating words).

## [N-tr-03] Case usage — accusative definiteness

Confirm the accusative (-i) is used only for definite objects, and the other
cases (dative/locative/ablative/genitive) are correct.

- [ ] Case usage correct; accusative definiteness right.

## [N-tr-04] Verb morphology & evidential -miş

Confirm tense/aspect/person suffixes, and especially the -di (witnessed) vs
-miş (reported/inferred) distinction in example sentences.

- [ ] Verb forms + evidential -miş nuance correct.

## [N-tr-05] Stress placement

Confirm stress is on the correct syllable for lesson words (usually final, but
place names and some suffixes shift it: Ankara, -iyor).

- [ ] Stress correct for lesson vocabulary.

## [N-tr-06] sen / siz naturalness + Bey/Hanım

Confirm the informal/formal choice reads naturally and Bey/Hanım placement
(after first name) is correct in every example.

- [ ] T-V choice + Bey/Hanım natural.

## [N-tr-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm sentences sound natural and contemporary, SOV order is
natural, and politeness formulas (rica ederim, teşekkür ederim) are idiomatic.

- [ ] Sentences natural; SOV + politeness idiomatic.
