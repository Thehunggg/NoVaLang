# nb — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Norwegian Bokmål (nb-NO) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure
Norwegian language facts the project owner (not a Norwegian speaker) cannot
verify. None block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine
confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-nb-01] en/ei/et gender for common vocabulary

Gender is largely lexical; Bokmål allows merging masc+fem into common (en bok /
ei bok). Confirm the article(s) for the first lesson's vocabulary.

- [ ] en/ei/et correct (and merge variant acceptable) for lesson-1 nouns.

## [N-nb-02] Definite suffix + double definiteness

Confirm the definite suffix (-en/-a/-et/-ene) and double definiteness with
adjectives (den røde bilen — article AND suffix, unlike Danish).

- [ ] Definite + double-definite forms correct.

## [N-nb-03] Pitch accent (tonelag)

Pitch accent distinguishes meaning (bønder/bønner, tanken). Confirm it is
correct in audio for lesson vocabulary where it matters.

- [ ] Pitch accent correct in audio examples.

## [N-nb-04] Pronunciation vs spelling (silent letters, sj/kj)

Confirm the audio pronunciation (hv→v, silent d/g finals, sj-lyd [ʂ], kj-lyd
[ç]) is correct for lesson words.

- [ ] Audio pronunciation correct.

## [N-nb-05] Strong/irregular verbs

Confirm strong verbs (skrive–skrev–skrevet) and være/ha/gå tables.

- [ ] Core strong-verb list verified.

## [N-nb-06] du naturalness

Confirm du reads as natural everywhere and no example forces an archaic De.

- [ ] du natural; no forced De.

## [N-nb-07] Bokmål (not Nynorsk) + naturalness / register

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences are natural Bokmål (not Nynorsk forms),
V2 order correct, article placement (den røde bilen).

- [ ] Sentences natural Bokmål; V2 + article placement correct.
