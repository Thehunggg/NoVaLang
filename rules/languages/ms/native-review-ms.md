# ms — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Malay (ms-MY) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Malay language
facts the project owner (not a Malay speaker) cannot verify. **Especially
important because there is NO Malay corpus available** — rules rest on general
knowledge + standard descriptions + Indonesian cross-reference, not Malay
real-data validation. None block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status;
they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-ms-01] meN- nasal assimilation

Confirm the meN- prefix nasal form (men-/mem-/meng-/meny-/menge-) is correct for
lesson verbs.

- [ ] meN- forms correct.

## [N-ms-02] Affixes + reduplication

Confirm ber-/di-/ter-/peN-, suffixes -kan/-i/-an, ke-...-an, and reduplication
(buku-buku) are correct for lesson vocabulary.

- [ ] Affixes + reduplication correct.

## [N-ms-03] Classifiers (penjodoh bilangan)

Confirm the correct classifier ([number]+[classifier]+[noun]: tiga buah buku).

- [ ] Classifiers correct.

## [N-ms-04] Tense/aspect words

Confirm sudah/akan/sedang/telah/belum are used correctly and naturally.

- [ ] Tense/aspect words correct.

## [N-ms-05] 'e' pronunciation ([e] vs schwa) in audio

'e' is [e] (emak) or schwa [ə] (beli), unmarked. Confirm audio pronunciation is
correct for lesson words.

- [ ] 'e' pronunciation correct in audio.

## [N-ms-06] saya/anda + register; not Indonesian

Confirm pronoun/register (saya/anda/awak, encik/puan) is natural Malay and no
Indonesianisms slipped in (wang not uang, standard ms-MY).

- [ ] Natural Malay register; no Indonesianisms.

## [N-ms-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm sentences sound natural Malay, SVO + modifier-after-noun
correct, register appropriate. (No corpus was available — this review is the
main quality gate.)

- [ ] Sentences natural; grammar + register correct.
