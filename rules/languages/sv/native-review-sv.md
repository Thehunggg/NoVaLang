# sv — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Swedish (sv-SE) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Swedish
language facts the project owner (not a Swedish speaker) cannot verify. None
block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-sv-01] en/ett gender for common vocabulary

en (common) vs ett (neuter) is largely lexical (~75% en). Confirm the article
for the first lesson's vocabulary once real content exists.

- [ ] en/ett correct for lesson-1 nouns.

## [N-sv-02] Definite suffix + plural forms

Confirm the definite suffix (-en/-et/-n/-t) and plural forms (-ar/-er/-or/-n/
none + definite -na/-en) for lesson vocabulary; these vary by declension class.

- [ ] Definite + plural forms correct.

## [N-sv-03] Strong/irregular verb list

Strong verbs (vowel-changing: skriva–skrev–skrivit) and vara/ha/gå are
irregular. Confirm a compact tense table before verb exercises ship.

- [ ] Core strong-verb list verified.

## [N-sv-04] Pitch accent (accent 1 vs 2)

Confirm accent assignment for lesson vocabulary where it distinguishes meaning
(anden duck/spirit, tomten). Not essential for beginners but flag audio.

- [ ] Pitch accent correct in audio examples.

## [N-sv-05] Front-vowel softening exceptions

k/g/sk soften before front vowels in native words but stay hard in many
loanwords (kex, geografi). Confirm example words apply the right pronunciation.

- [ ] Softening vs loanword-hard correct.

## [N-sv-06] du/ni naturalness

Confirm du reads as natural everywhere (including service/formal contexts) and
that no example forces an archaic formal ni.

- [ ] du natural; no forced archaic ni.

## [N-sv-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences sound natural to a native (not literal),
V2 order correct, double definiteness idiomatic.

- [ ] Sentences natural; V2 + double definiteness correct.
