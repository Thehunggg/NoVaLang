# th — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Thai (th-TH, Central/Bangkok) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure
Thai language facts the project owner (not a Thai speaker) cannot verify. None
block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-th-01] Tones in audio

Thai has 5 tones (meaning-distinguishing). Confirm the tone of each lesson word
is correct in the audio.

- [ ] Tones correct in audio.

## [N-th-02] Vowel/tone-mark spelling

Confirm the spelling (vowel placement around consonant, tone marks) is correct
for lesson vocabulary.

- [ ] Spelling (vowels + tone marks) correct.

## [N-th-03] Classifiers

Confirm the correct classifier for each counted noun ([noun]+[number]+
[classifier]).

- [ ] Classifiers correct for lesson nouns.

## [N-th-04] Tense/aspect particles

Confirm particles (จะ/แล้ว/กำลัง/เคย) are used correctly and naturally.

- [ ] Tense/aspect particles correct.

## [N-th-05] Pronouns + politeness particles (gender)

Confirm pronouns (ผม/ดิฉัน/คุณ…) and the sentence-final particle (ครับ male /
ค่ะ female) match the speaker's gender and register.

- [ ] Pronouns + ครับ/ค่ะ correct for speaker gender.

## [N-th-06] Word segmentation / spacing

Confirm phrase/sentence spacing looks natural (no spaces inside words) and text
segments correctly for display/TTS.

- [ ] Spacing/segmentation natural.

## [N-th-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm sentences sound natural Central Thai, no royal/temple
vocabulary misused, modifier-after-noun order correct.

- [ ] Sentences natural; register appropriate.
