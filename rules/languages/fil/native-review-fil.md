# fil — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Filipino (Tagalog) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Filipino
language facts the project owner (not a Filipino speaker) cannot verify.
**Especially important because the corpus is very small (222 sentences)** — many
rules rest on general knowledge, not large real-data validation. None block the
current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-fil-01] Verb focus / trigger system

Confirm the focus affix (-um-/mag- actor vs -in/i-/-an object) matches the
ang/ng marking in lesson sentences.

- [ ] Focus affixes + ang/ng marking correct.

## [N-fil-02] Affixation + reduplication

Confirm infixes (s-um-ulat), reduplication for aspect (susulat 'will write'),
and prefixes/suffixes are correct.

- [ ] Affixation + reduplication correct.

## [N-fil-03] Marker particles ang/ng/sa

Confirm ang/ng/sa (and si/ni/kay for names) are used correctly.

- [ ] Marker particles correct.

## [N-fil-04] po / ho respect particle

Confirm po/ho placement and usage are natural and appropriate to the
relationship (respect for elders).

- [ ] po/ho natural and correctly placed.

## [N-fil-05] Stress / glottal stop in audio

Stress and glottal stop are usually unwritten but meaning-distinguishing
(áso/asó). Confirm they are correct in audio.

- [ ] Stress + glottal stop correct in audio.

## [N-fil-06] Standard Filipino (not Taglish) + accents

Confirm sentences are natural standard Filipino (not forced Taglish) and that
omitting accents reads as normal (accents optional).

- [ ] Natural standard Filipino; accent policy correct.

## [N-fil-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm sentences sound natural, VSO/linker correct, register
(po/ho) appropriate.

- [ ] Sentences natural; word order + register correct.
