# nl — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Dutch (nl-NL) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Dutch language
facts the project owner (not a Dutch speaker) cannot verify. None block the
current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-nl-01] de/het gender for common vocabulary

de vs het is largely lexical (~2/3 de). Confirm the article for the first
lesson's vocabulary once real content exists.

- [ ] de/het correct for lesson-1 nouns.

## [N-nl-02] Diminutive suffix selection

The -je/-tje/-etje/-pje/-kje choice follows the stem's final sound. Confirm the
diminutive form for lesson vocabulary (huisje, boompje, mannetje, koninkje).

- [ ] Diminutive forms correct.

## [N-nl-03] Strong/irregular verb list

Strong verbs (vowel-changing) and hebben/zijn/gaan/zullen are irregular.
Confirm a compact conjugation table before verb exercises ship.

- [ ] Core strong-verb list verified.

## [N-nl-04] Separable verbs

Confirm which verbs are separable and that example sentences place the prefix
correctly (Ik sta om 7 uur op / Ik ben opgestaan).

- [ ] Separable-verb placement correct.

## [N-nl-05] Adjective inflection (-e) edge cases

Confirm the -e rule, especially the het-word indefinite exception (een mooi
huis / het mooie huis / een mooie man).

- [ ] Adjective -e correct in examples.

## [N-nl-06] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences sound natural to a native (nl-NL, not
Flemish), and je/u choice fits each situation.

- [ ] Sentences natural; je/u appropriate.
