# it — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Italian speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Italian language
facts the project owner (not an Italian speaker) cannot verify. None block the
current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-it-01] Auxiliary essere vs avere verb list

Which verbs take *essere* (not *avere*) in compound tenses is largely lexical.
Confirm a compact list (andare, venire, essere, stare, nascere, morire,
diventare, reflexives...) before compound-tense exercises ship.

- [ ] essere-verb list correct.

## [N-it-02] Noun gender for common vocabulary

Gender (m/f) and irregular plurals (la mano, il problema, l'uovo/le uova) must
be verified per noun once real content exists.

- [ ] Gender + plural correct for lesson-1 nouns.

## [N-it-03] Definite article by initial sound

Confirm article choice for edge cases: lo/gli before s+consonant, z, gn, ps,
pn, x, y, and semivowel i (lo iodio / lo yogurt).

- [ ] Article correct in examples.

## [N-it-04] Written accent placement

Confirm grave vs acute on final stressed vowels (perché acute, caffè grave)
and homograph accents (e/è, da/dà, se/sé) in example sentences.

- [ ] Accents correct.

## [N-it-05] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences sound natural, and tu/Lei choice fits
each situation.

- [ ] Sentences natural; tu/Lei appropriate.
