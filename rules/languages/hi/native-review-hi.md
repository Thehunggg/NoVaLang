# hi — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Hindi (hi-IN) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Hindi language
facts the project owner (not a Hindi speaker) cannot verify. None block the
current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-hi-01] Gender & adjective agreement

Confirm gender (masc/fem) for lesson nouns and adjective ending agreement
(अच्छा/अच्छी/अच्छे), including exceptions not predictable from the ending.

- [ ] Gender + adjective agreement correct.

## [N-hi-02] Oblique forms & postpositions

Confirm the oblique noun form before postpositions (लड़का→लड़के) and correct
postposition usage (ने/को/से/में/पर, का/के/की agreement).

- [ ] Oblique forms + postpositions correct.

## [N-hi-03] Ergative construction

Confirm perfective transitive sentences use ने and the verb agrees with the
object gender/number (राम ने रोटी खाई), and non-perfective does not.

- [ ] Ergative ने + object agreement correct.

## [N-hi-04] Verb conjugation & honorific agreement

Confirm tense/aspect forms, होना auxiliary agreement, and that आप takes plural
verb forms.

- [ ] Verb forms + आप plural agreement correct.

## [N-hi-05] Schwa deletion & retroflex in audio

Confirm the audio deletes the inherent schwa correctly (राम=[raːm] not [raːmə])
and retroflex consonants (ट/ड) sound correct.

- [ ] Schwa deletion + retroflex correct in audio.

## [N-hi-06] तू / तुम / आप naturalness

Confirm the honorific level fits each example's relationship (आप with strangers,
तू not used where it would sound rude) and जी usage is natural.

- [ ] Honorific level + जी natural in every example.

## [N-hi-07] Naturalness / standard Hindi register

Per ADR-016: confirm sentences sound natural in standard Hindi — not overly
Sanskritized nor overly Urdu-ized — SOV order natural, politeness formulas
(कृपया, धन्यवाद, माफ़ कीजिए) idiomatic.

- [ ] Sentences natural standard Hindi; politeness idiomatic.
