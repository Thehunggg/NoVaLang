# ro — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Romanian (ro-RO) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Romanian
language facts the project owner (not a Romanian speaker) cannot verify. None
block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-ro-01] Noun gender (incl. neuter) + plural

Gender including the neuter (masc sing / fem plural) and plural endings
(-i/-e/-uri with stem alternations: carte→cărți, masă→mese) are lexical.
Confirm for lesson-1 nouns.

- [ ] Gender + plural correct.

## [N-ro-02] Definite article suffix forms

Confirm the postposed definite article (-ul/-l/-a/-i/-le) is correct per
gender/number for lesson vocabulary.

- [ ] Definite suffix correct.

## [N-ro-03] Genitive-dative forms

Confirm gen-dat marking (on article + some endings: casei, băiatului) in
example sentences.

- [ ] Gen-dat correct.

## [N-ro-04] Subjunctive (să) vs infinitive

Confirm example sentences use subjunctive (vreau să merg) where Romanian
prefers it over the infinitive.

- [ ] Subjunctive usage idiomatic.

## [N-ro-05] Clitic doubling + object pronoun placement

Confirm object clitic placement (îl văd) and clitic doubling (îl văd pe Ion)
sound natural.

- [ ] Clitic + doubling correct.

## [N-ro-06] â/î spelling + ș/ț comma-below

Confirm â/î spelling follows the 1993 rule and that ș/ț use comma-below (not
cedilla) in all content.

- [ ] â/î + comma-below correct.

## [N-ro-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences sound natural (not literal), tu/dvs
appropriate, subjunctive/article idiomatic.

- [ ] Sentences natural; register + morphology idiomatic.
