# id — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Indonesian (id-ID) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Indonesian
language facts the project owner (not an Indonesian speaker) cannot verify. None
block the current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-id-01] Affix forms & meN- nasalization

Confirm the meN- nasalization (menulis, membaca, menyapu, mengirim) and other
affixes (ber-, di-, -kan, -i, ke-...-an) are correct for lesson vocabulary.

- [ ] Affix + meN- nasalization correct for lesson-1 words.

## [N-id-02] Reduplication

Confirm reduplicated forms and their meaning (plural buku-buku, iterative
jalan-jalan, fixed kupu-kupu) are correct and natural.

- [ ] Reduplication forms + meaning correct.

## [N-id-03] Schwa 'e' pronunciation

Confirm which 'e' is [ə] (schwa) vs [e] for lesson words in the audio (emas
[ə] vs enak [e]) — spelling does not distinguish them.

- [ ] Schwa/[e] correct in audio for lesson vocabulary.

## [N-id-04] Pronoun register & clusivity

Confirm pronoun choices fit the register (saya/aku, Anda/kamu) and kami
(exclusive) vs kita (inclusive) are used correctly.

- [ ] Pronoun register + kami/kita correct.

## [N-id-05] Word order & passive

Confirm adjective-after-noun (rumah besar), possessive order (buku saya), and
di- passive usage are natural.

- [ ] Word order + passive natural.

## [N-id-06] Tense/aspect particles

Confirm sudah/sedang/akan/belum/masih are used correctly to express time
(no verb inflection).

- [ ] Aspect particles used correctly.

## [N-id-07] Naturalness / baku vs gaul

Per ADR-016: confirm sentences sound natural in bahasa baku (standard), not
awkwardly formal or unintentionally gaul (slang), and politeness formulas
(tolong, terima kasih, permisi) are idiomatic.

- [ ] Sentences natural bahasa baku; politeness idiomatic.
