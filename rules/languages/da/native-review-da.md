# da — Native speaker review checklist

For a native Danish (da-DK) speaker. Simple tick-box items. Pure Danish language
facts the project owner (not a Danish speaker) cannot verify. None block the
current VALIDATED_NOT_YET_PROVEN status; they refine confidence.

No front-matter (native-review file, exempt).

---

## [N-da-01] en/et gender for common vocabulary

en (common) vs et (neuter) is largely lexical (~75% en). Confirm the article
for the first lesson's vocabulary.

- [ ] en/et correct for lesson-1 nouns.

## [N-da-02] Definite suffix + plural forms

Confirm the definite suffix (-en/-et/-ne) and plural forms (-er/-e/-∅) for
lesson vocabulary.

- [ ] Definite + plural forms correct.

## [N-da-03] stød assignment

stød distinguishes meaning (mor/mord, hun/hund). Confirm stød is correct in
audio for lesson vocabulary where it matters.

- [ ] stød correct in audio examples.

## [N-da-04] Pronunciation vs spelling (soft d, silent letters)

Danish spelling is opaque. Confirm the audio pronunciation (soft d, silent/
reduced finals) is correct for lesson words — this is the top priority for da.

- [ ] Audio pronunciation correct (not spelling-based).

## [N-da-05] Strong/irregular verbs

Confirm strong verbs (skrive–skrev–skrevet) and være/have/gå tables.

- [ ] Core strong-verb list verified.

## [N-da-06] du naturalness

Confirm du reads as natural everywhere and no example forces an archaic De.

- [ ] du natural; no forced De.

## [N-da-07] Naturalness / register of translations

Per ADR-016: confirm example sentences sound natural, V2 order correct, article
placement (den røde bil, not double definiteness).

- [ ] Sentences natural; V2 + article placement correct.
